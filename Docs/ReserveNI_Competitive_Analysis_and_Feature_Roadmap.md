# ReserveNI — Competitive Analysis & Feature Roadmap

**Prepared: March 2026**
**Purpose:** Identify competitors, map their features, and define a comprehensive feature set that positions ReserveNI to meet or exceed the competition for independent restaurants in Northern Ireland.

---

## 1. Competitive Landscape

### Tier 1 — Direct Competitors (UK/Ireland focused, targeting independents)

**ResDiary** (The Access Group)
Powers 9,000+ venues globally. Strong UK/Ireland presence. Commission-free, flat monthly fee (£89–£245/month depending on reservation volume). Integrates with 60+ partners including EPoS, PMS, and marketing tools. Offers its own diner discovery platform (Dish Cult). Core strengths: yield management, table management, event enquiry handling, gift vouchers, and online ordering. Weaknesses noted in reviews: complex setup, unintuitive UI for non-technical users, reporting needs improvement, mixed customer support experiences, and many features (POS integration, online ordering, gift vouchers) are paid add-ons.

**Dojo Bookings** (Paymentsense/Dojo)
UK-focused system tied to the Dojo payments ecosystem. No per-cover fees; transparent monthly pricing. Key differentiator: virtual queuing system that lets walk-ins join a queue via QR code and receive SMS when their table is ready. Integrates with Reserve with Google and the Dojo Dining Experiences app (850,000+ users). Includes deposits, pre-orders, guest profiles, automated email/SMS, and table management. Weakness: must use Dojo for card payments; feature depth is lighter than dedicated platforms.

**Tableo**
Newer platform, strong in Malta and expanding in the UK. Free tier available (up to 100 covers/month). Integrates with MICHELIN Guide, TripAdvisor, and Reserve with Google. AI chatbot on Facebook Messenger and Instagram that takes bookings, answers questions, and handles cancellations in any language 24/7. No per-cover fees. Offers floor plan management, guest profiles, no-show fee collection, and automated reminders. Weakness: smaller user network, limited traffic generation, some mobile usability issues.

### Tier 2 — Major Global Platforms

**OpenTable**
Market leader globally. 60,000+ restaurants, 33 million diners seated monthly, 1.8 billion annually. Largest diner network in the world. Plans from ~$149/month + $1–$1.50 per cover. Comprehensive feature set: table management with auto-assignment, AI-powered review responses, virtual waitlists, Experiences (ticketed events, tasting menus, chef's tables), POS integration (30+ providers), benchmark reporting against local competitors, 200+ integrations, owner/manager mobile app, private dining lead management, direct messaging, marketing campaigns with bonus points for off-peak. Weaknesses: expensive per-cover fees that add up quickly, restaurants don't fully own guest data, one-year contracts, overkill for smaller independents.

**SevenRooms**
Premium all-in-one CRM, marketing, and operations platform. No diner marketplace — focuses on direct bookings through the restaurant's own channels. White-labelled everything. Full guest data ownership. Auto-tagging system (VIP, Wine Lover, Dairy Allergy, etc.), AI-powered auto-seating algorithm, marketing automation (email campaigns, SMS), reputation management dashboard aggregating online reviews, ticketed events and experiences, multi-venue management with cross-property guest data sharing, "Priority Alerts" for VIPs when last-minute slots open, "Private Line" SMS concierge for top guests. Pricing is quote-based and reportedly starts around $6,000/year. Weakness: enterprise-level complexity and cost; better suited to large groups than small independents.

**Tock** (now owned by Squarespace)
Pioneered the prepayment/ticketing model for restaurants. Zero per-cover fees, flat monthly pricing ($79–$769/month across four tiers). Strong in experiences and events: prepaid tasting menus, cooking classes, ticketed special nights. Full CRM with guest profiles, visit history, dietary restrictions. Integrates with Google, social media, Toast POS. Marketplace for diner discovery. Weakness: ticketing model can deter casual diners; email marketing and auto-tagging not as robust as SevenRooms; POS integrations limited.

**Resy** (owned by American Express)
Flat monthly pricing ($249–$899/month). Strong editorial content and trending restaurant curation. Exclusive benefits for Amex cardholders. CRM features, guest surveys (not on basic plan), events ticketing. Has exited the UK market, making it less relevant but worth noting for feature inspiration. Weakness: limited CRM (month-to-month guest data only), data shared with Resy for their own marketing, expensive entry point.

### Tier 3 — Other Notable Players

**TheFork Manager** (TripAdvisor group) — Strong in Europe, marketplace access to millions of diners, promotional tools, deposits, and card capture. Not dominant in NI/UK.

**Quandoo** — Popular in Germany/Asia, simple interface, map-based search, cashback rewards for diners. Lightweight feature set.

**Eat App** — Cloud-based, strong in Middle East/Asia. Good CRM, AI wait time predictions, no-show risk scoring, free plan available (30 covers). Good feature-to-price ratio.

**resOS** — Scandinavian platform, free plan with 25 bookings/month. Simple, affordable.

**TableAgent** — Completely free, basic cloud reservation management. No frills.

**Access Collins** (DesignMyNight) — UK-focused, strong for pubs/bars/experience venues. Access to DesignMyNight's 3.9 million monthly users.

## 3. Recommended Feature Set for ReserveNI

Based on this analysis, here is a comprehensive feature list organised by priority. Features are grouped into what you should have at launch (MVP+), what to build soon after (Phase 2), and what would make ReserveNI genuinely differentiated (Phase 3).

### 3.1 — MVP+ Features (Launch Readiness)

These are table-stakes features that every competitor offers. ReserveNI already covers most of these.

1. **Online booking widget** — Embeddable on venue websites via iFrame or JS snippet. Mobile-responsive, brandable with venue colours/logo. *(In progress)*
2. **Hosted booking page** — Standalone URL for venues without websites. Shareable on social media. *(In progress)*
3. **Availability engine** — Fixed time intervals and named sittings (lunch, dinner). Configurable covers-per-slot. *(In progress)*
4. **Deposit collection** — Via Stripe Connect, configurable per venue (amount, when required). *(In progress)*
5. **Email confirmations and reminders** — Automated on booking, 24h before, and customisable templates. *(In progress)*
6. **SMS confirmations and reminders** — Via Twilio. Booking confirmation + day-before reminder. *(In progress)*
7. **Reservations dashboard** — Unified view of all bookings (online + phone). Filter by date, status, sitting. *(In progress)*
8. **Phone booking entry** — Staff can manually add phone/walk-in bookings with optional deposit request sent via SMS/email. *(In progress)*
9. **Day sheet view** — Printable shift overview showing all bookings for a service with guest notes, party sizes, special requirements. *(In progress)*
10. **Venue onboarding** — Self-service setup wizard: opening hours, sittings, tables, deposit rules. *(In progress)*
11. **QR code generation** — For table-top or window display linking to the booking page. *(In progress)*
12. **Basic reporting** — Covers per day/week/month, booking source breakdown, no-show rate, revenue from deposits.
13. **Guest identity matching** — Merge repeat guests by phone/email to build visit history over time. *(In progress)*
14. **Cancellation and modification** — Guest self-service links in confirmation emails to cancel or modify. Configurable cancellation policy (e.g., 24h notice or forfeit deposit).
15. **Stripe Connect onboarding** — Venues connect their own Stripe account for direct deposit collection. *(In progress)*
16. **Commission-free, flat-fee pricing** — This is your strongest strategic position. Every indie restaurant hates per-cover fees. Lead with this.

### 3.2 — Phase 2 Features (Post-Launch, First 3–6 Months)

These are the features that separate a functional booking tool from a genuinely useful restaurant management platform.

17. **Guest profiles / lightweight CRM** — Automatic profile creation from bookings. Track visit count, total spend (when POS connected), dietary notes, allergies, special occasions (birthdays, anniversaries), and free-text notes from staff. Display profile at time of booking and on day sheet.
18. **Guest tagging** — Manual tags first (VIP, Regular, Difficult, Press, Influencer, etc.). This is one of the most valued features across all competitors. Even simple tagging transforms how front-of-house staff greet and serve guests.
19. **Reserve with Google** — Becoming essential for discovery. 73% of Reserve with Google bookings come from first-time diners (per Tableo's 2024 data). This is a major acquisition channel that ReserveNI should integrate as early as possible.
20. **Table management / floor plan** — Visual drag-and-drop floor plan builder. Map tables to areas (main dining, terrace, private room). Show real-time occupancy during service. This doesn't need to be as sophisticated as OpenTable's initially — even a simple grid view with table status (available, occupied, reserved) adds enormous value.
21. **Waitlist management** — When fully booked, allow guests to join a digital waitlist. Notify by SMS when a table opens. Even a simple implementation differentiates from paper-diary competitors.
22. **Walk-in logging** — Quick-add for walk-ins so the day's data is complete. Captures covers even without a reservation.
23. **Auto-cancellation for unconfirmed bookings** — If a guest doesn't confirm via reminder link within X hours, auto-cancel and free the slot. Configurable per venue. *(Partially in progress via Vercel cron jobs)*
24. **Customisable booking form fields** — Let venues add custom questions: "Any allergies?", "Celebrating a special occasion?", "High chair needed?", "Wheelchair access required?"
25. **Multi-channel booking source tracking** — Tag where each booking originated (website widget, hosted page, phone, social media, Google). Report on channel performance.
26. **Enhanced reporting** — Revenue per available seat hour (RevPASH), peak vs off-peak utilisation, average party size trends, booking lead time analysis, cancellation rate by source.
27. **Booking notes for staff** — Internal notes attached to reservations visible on the day sheet (e.g., "Regular — prefers corner table", "Reviewing for Belfast Telegraph").
28. **Automated post-dining feedback request** — Send a short satisfaction survey or Google Review prompt 2 hours after the reservation time. Simple thumbs up/down + optional comment. This is low-effort, high-value.

### 3.3 — Phase 3 Features (6–12 Months, Differentiation)

These features would make ReserveNI genuinely competitive with the mid-tier platforms and potentially superior for the NI independent restaurant market.

29. **Email marketing (basic)** — Let venues send simple campaigns to their guest database: "We've launched a new menu", "Book your Christmas party", "Last-minute tables tonight". Template-based, no design skills needed. Even basic Mailchimp-style functionality built into the platform would be a huge differentiator vs ResDiary (which charges extra) and Dojo (which doesn't offer it at all).
30. **Special events / experiences** — Allow venues to create bookable experiences: tasting menus, wine pairing nights, chef's table, New Year's Eve set menus, Sunday roast specials. These can carry prepayment (full or deposit). Tock built its entire business on this. It's increasingly important as experiential dining grows.
31. **Gift vouchers** — Digital gift vouchers purchasable through the venue's booking page. Redeemable against deposits or presented at the venue. ResDiary charges extra for this. Most competitors don't offer it at all. Simple to implement with Stripe and extremely popular with consumers.
32. **Pre-order capability** — Let guests pre-select from set menus or add items (bottle of wine for the table, sharing platter for arrival) at booking time. Improves kitchen prep, increases average spend, and reduces service friction.
33. **Automated no-show tracking and flagging** — Track guests who repeatedly no-show. Auto-flag them on future bookings so staff can require deposits or contact to confirm. This is one of the most requested features in restaurant forums.
34. **Dynamic deposit rules** — Require deposits only at peak times (Friday/Saturday dinner), for large parties (6+), or for flagged no-show guests. Weekday lunch? No deposit needed. This flexibility is something many systems lack.
35. **Multi-venue support** — Allow restaurant groups to manage multiple venues from one login. Shared guest database across venues. Group-level reporting. Even in NI, many operators run 2–3 sites.
36. **Owner/manager mobile app or PWA** — A simple mobile-optimised dashboard: today's bookings at a glance, push notifications for new bookings, ability to confirm/reject/modify from phone. OpenTable's owner app is one of its most-loved features.
37. **Reputation management** — Aggregate Google Reviews, TripAdvisor ratings, and survey feedback in one dashboard. Help venues respond to reviews faster. SevenRooms does this well.
38. **Yield management** — Adjust available covers per time slot based on demand patterns. If Thursday at 7pm is always full, reduce online availability to hold slots for phone bookings or walk-ins. If Tuesday at 6pm is always empty, open extra slots.

## 4. Innovative / Differentiating Feature Ideas

These are features that most competitors don't offer well (or at all) that could give ReserveNI a genuine edge, particularly for the NI market.

### 4.1 — "NI-First" Local Features

**Local Discovery Feed** — A simple public directory of ReserveNI venues. Not trying to be OpenTable's marketplace, but a "Book a table in Belfast / Derry / Bangor tonight" page that surfaces participating restaurants with real-time availability. Low cost to build, high value for venues, and a strong marketing tool for the platform itself.

**"Last-Minute Tables" Widget** — A shared widget or page showing tonight's available tables across all ReserveNI venues. Restaurants opt-in to share unsold inventory. Think of it as a local version of what Dojo's app does — helps fill empty seats at the last minute.

### 4.2 — Operations Intelligence

**Smart Staffing Hints** — Based on booking data, show venues a simple "Expected covers" forecast for coming days/weeks. "This Saturday looks 40% busier than last Saturday — consider extra staff." No AI needed — just trend comparison against previous weeks. Restaurants would love this.

**Service Pace Tracker** — Let front-of-house mark when guests are seated, when mains are served, and when the table is cleared. Over time, this builds average turn-time data per sitting, party size, and day of week. Helps venues understand their actual capacity vs theoretical capacity.

**Weather-Adjusted Forecasting** — Cross-reference booking patterns with weather data. Rainy weekends in NI tend to drive more bookings; sunny days push people to beer gardens. A simple "Heads up: rain forecast this Saturday, expect higher demand" notification would feel magical.

### 4.3 — Guest Experience Enhancements

**Booking Confirmation Mini-Page** — Instead of a plain email, send a link to a beautiful confirmation page showing: the venue's location on a map, parking info, menu link, dress code, any pre-order options, and a "Add to Calendar" button. Every competitor sends boring text emails. This is a chance to delight.

**Guest Preference Memory** — When a repeat guest books, auto-populate their known preferences: "Last time you sat in the garden — would you like that again?", "You noted a nut allergy — we've flagged this for the kitchen." This is what SevenRooms does at the enterprise level. A simplified version for independents would be incredibly compelling.

**Occasion Reminders** — If a guest booked for a birthday last March, prompt the venue in February: "Sarah booked for her birthday last year — send her an invite?" Simple CRM automation that makes the restaurant look thoughtful and drives repeat bookings.

### 4.4 — Revenue Maximisers

**Upsell at Booking** — Offer add-ons during the booking flow: "Add a bottle of Prosecco for the table (£25)", "Pre-order our sharing board (£18)". Collected via Stripe at booking time. Increases average transaction value and reduces kitchen surprise orders.

**Dynamic Pricing Signals** — Not dynamic pricing itself (which restaurants hate), but visibility signals: "High demand" badges on popular slots, and "Great availability" on quiet ones. Nudges diners toward off-peak times without changing prices. TheFork does this with discounts; ReserveNI could do it with gentle framing.

**"Confirm or Lose" Countdown** — For high-demand slots, if a guest hasn't confirmed 24h before, send a final SMS: "Your table at [Venue] tomorrow at 7pm hasn't been confirmed. Confirm now or your booking will be released in 2 hours." Creates urgency, reduces no-shows, and frees up tables for the waitlist.

### 4.5 — Platform Simplicity as a Feature

**One-Page Setup** — Most competitors require extensive configuration. ReserveNI could differentiate by letting a venue go from "zero to accepting bookings" in under 10 minutes. A single-page onboarding wizard: venue name, opening hours, number of covers per sitting, deposit yes/no, done. Polish later.

**Plain-Language Dashboard** — Avoid jargon. Instead of "RevPASH" show "Revenue per seat per hour." Instead of "Yield management" show "How full are you?" ResDiary's complexity is a common complaint. ReserveNI's simplicity can be a feature, not a limitation.

**WhatsApp Booking Confirmation** — In NI, WhatsApp is ubiquitous. Offering booking confirmations and reminders via WhatsApp (in addition to SMS and email) would feel modern and reduce SMS costs. WhatsApp Business API supports template messages for exactly this use case.

**Staff Handover Notes** — A simple feature where the closing manager can leave notes for the opening team: "Table 6 has a wobbly leg", "VIP booking at 8pm — Mr. Campbell, allergic to shellfish, prefers window seat." Attached to the day's shift, visible on the day sheet. No competitor does this well.

## 5. Strategic Positioning Summary

ReserveNI's competitive advantage for NI independents comes from combining these principles:

**Price transparency** — Flat monthly fee, no per-cover charges, no commission. This alone will win conversations with restaurants burned by OpenTable's model or intimidated by ResDiary's add-on pricing.

**Simplicity** — Built for owner-operators who don't have a tech team. If the chef-owner can set it up between services, you win. Every competitor review mentions complexity as a pain point.

**Local focus** — Purpose-built for the NI market. Understanding that a 40-seat gastropub in Holywood has different needs than a 200-cover London brasserie. Local support, local understanding.

**Deposit-first design** — No-shows are the single biggest operational pain point for NI restaurants. ReserveNI's deposit-forward approach (powered by Stripe Connect, going directly to the venue's account) is a strong differentiator when most systems treat deposits as an afterthought or a premium add-on.

**Growth path** — Start simple, but build toward the features that matter: guest profiles, marketing tools, event booking, and reporting. The roadmap above gives you 12+ months of meaningful development that keeps venues engaged and growing with the platform.
